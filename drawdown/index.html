The rules being animated
  • Intraday trailing (Apex-style): the floor sits a fixed distance below your highest equity ever reached — including unrealized profit on open trades. It moves up the moment equity ticks a new high, and never comes back down. On most firms it locks once it reaches your starting balance.
  • EOD trailing (Topstep / MFFU-style): the floor recalculates once per day from your closing balance. Paper profits during the day don't move it. Far more forgiving on the exact same trades.
  • Daily loss limit (Topstep): lose a set amount in one day (including unrealized) and you're flat until tomorrow — a soft breach, not a failed eval.
  • Scenario numbers use a $50K account with a $2,000 trailing drawdown and $1,000 DLL. Verify current rules on the firm's official page.
